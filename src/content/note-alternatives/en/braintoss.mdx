## What Braintoss Does Well

Let's be honest. For what it does, Braintoss is excellent — and it's been doing the same thing well for over a decade.

What stands out:

- **Speed.** One tap, speak / snap / scribble, sent. The whole loop is faster than opening any other notes app
- **One-time pricing.** $2.99 on iOS, $3.49 on Android. Pay once, use for years
- **Email as the integration.** Every tool reads email — Things, Todoist, Notion, Evernote. Braintoss feeds them all without a single Zapier rule
- **Three input modes.** Voice + photo + scribble — not just voice
- **Apple Watch support** for capture on the wrist
- **Still actively maintained** — iOS update 2025-10-14 with iOS 26 fixes
- **Domain moved from braintoss.com to braintoss.app** — same product, just a rename

If your workflow is "capture fast, process in the tool I actually live in," Braintoss is the right tool and you should stop reading.

## Where Slax Note Goes Different

We didn't build a faster capture tool. We built a different product for what happens *after* capture.

A lot of users we talk to capture into Braintoss (or Apple Notes, or Drafts), then spend the next 20 minutes pasting the transcript into ChatGPT, asking for a tweet version, then a LinkedIn version, then a clean note. That second part — turning raw voice into shaped output — is the actual work.

### One recording, many shapes

You speak for two minutes. Then you pick a Prompt:

- *"Turn this into a 280-character tweet, sharp opener."*

- *"Rewrite as a blog draft with subheads."*
- *"Summarize as bullet points for my own notes."*

Same recording, four outputs, side by side. You keep what you want.

Braintoss gets the raw text into your inbox in 5 seconds. Slax Note gets the *finished* outputs in 30 — and you don't paste anywhere.
