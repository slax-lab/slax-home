There are a lot of read-later apps out there, and most of them are good at saving. Getting through what you saved is still your problem.

So instead of building more ways to organize your backlog, we focused on two things:

- **Save better**: Permanent archives, fast parsing, clean layouts — web articles, tweets, newsletters, embedded audio. Free, no expiration.
- **Read better**: Clickable AI outlines that let you move through long pieces without committing to the whole thing. Auto-tagging handles organization so you don't have to.

---

## How We Compare to the Most Common Alternatives

### [Slax Reader vs. Instapaper →](/reader/compare/slax-reader-vs-instapaper)

Still one of the cleanest save-and-read experiences out there. If you want more out of your reading time, here's how the two compare.

### Slax Reader vs. Readwise Reader *(coming soon)*

Readwise Reader is sold as a bundle — you pay for Readwise whether you use it or not. If web articles are most of what you read, it's worth asking whether you're paying for a lot you don't need.

### Slax Reader vs. Cubox *(coming soon)*

Cubox is genuinely good at organizing a large library, with folders, nested tags, smart filters, widgets. Whether that organizational overhead helps you read more, or just makes the backlog feel more tidy, depends on the person.

---

## A Note on Fairness

Readwise Reader handles PDFs, TTS, and third-party sync better than we do right now. Cubox has a more developed organizational system. Instapaper is genuinely good if minimalism is the point.

These comparisons are meant to help you figure out which tradeoffs matter for how you actually read.
