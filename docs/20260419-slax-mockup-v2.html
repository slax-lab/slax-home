01   What this is

Most reading apps help you save more. We'd rather help you finish what you saved, and remember what you read.

02   The products

Two tools, one quiet habit.

One reads in · one writes out

03   How people use it

Three afternoons, one way to work.

Scene 01
Reading

A long essay, caught on the subway.

You share a Paul Graham piece from your phone into Slax. On Monday, between meetings, it's already waiting — no ads, no paywall, no blue links. You highlight three paragraphs. One of them is the reason you'll quit your job, eventually. It stays highlighted forever.

Scene 02
Thinking

A question you can't answer.

Halfway through a paper on attention, you stop. You highlight the passage and ask the reader what it means in plainer terms. The answer sits in the margin, with a link back to the sentence that prompted it. No chat window. No new tab.

Scene 03
Writing

A Sunday, connecting the dots.

You open Slax Note. Last week's highlights are already there, grouped by what you read. You start a note called Why soft power is eroding. Two sentences in, Note surfaces a quote from Wednesday you'd forgotten. The essay writes itself. Almost.
